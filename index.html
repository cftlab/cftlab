We study what happens to transport when the flow changes the medium.

Our research is built on a single observation: transport in systems with flow-responsive boundaries and material properties cannot be described by classical models, and closing this gap requires new physics across membrane separation, biological flows, and complex mixing.

Direct numerical simulations, coupled multiphysics, and scientific machine learning — all realized on high-performance computing.

Research Program

Two thrusts. One physics.

Thrust I

Bioflow & Hemodynamics

How does flow history activate, damage, or signal through blood-borne macromolecules and cells — and what transport framework describes these outcomes when classical advection-diffusion and stress-based models fail?

Thrust II

Flow-Coupled Functional Surfaces

How does flow interact with functional surfaces — separative, reactive, stimuli-responsive — to regulate transport and surface-mediated processes, and how can that coupling be designed rather than merely optimized?

Shared Foundation

Beneath both thrusts: transport physics in variable-property, high-Schmidt-number, and turbulent flow regimes — viscosity-gradient-driven laminarization, turbulent mixing breakdown, and extreme-regime scalar transport — where classical closure models fail and new physical frameworks are required.
